About Me


"Computers aren’t the thing. They’re the thing that gets us to the thing." -- Joe MacMillan, Halt and Catch Fire

My name is Matthew Duran, and I am a second year Computer Science Master’s student at Columbia University. I am completing the Software Systems track, and through coursework and work experience I have garnered an appreciation for the precision required to engineer backend systems. While software is ingrained in every facet our daily lives, it is a powerful utility used by many to get to where they actually want to go. This fact is one of the reasons I am interested in full-stack development. This role offers me the ability to see projects go full circle, from engaging with lower level code that operates hardware to designing interfaces that bridge the gap between the underlying infrastructure and users. Whether the users are banking online or playing video games, it is great to see people leverage the power of modern computers through software.

I am a passionate, diligent, and curious developer. I don’t just stop with the first solution, but I iterate until an elegant solution is reached.
